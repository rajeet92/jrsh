Wheelchair Friendly Homes: Tips From Best Wheelchair Dealer in Gurgaon

People with disabilities already have a lot to deal with. Then, think about if they also have to face struggles while navigating in their home. Sounds quite troublesome, right? But there is a solution to such a dilemma. Incorporation of innovative solutions and clever renovation hacks can help to a great extent in making a house wheelchair-friendly. Thus, it is imperative that we make our homes accessible and accommodating for people who use wheelchairs so that they can move freely without facing any obstacles. But how do you renovate your home, keeping in mind the independence and comfort of wheelchair-using individuals? Here is how: 

Inspect your Living Area
Inspect all the areas of your living space. Determine any potential barriers and challenges that exist in the current scenario and assess how the different rooms are presently used. Such an evaluation will help you build the roadmap to making the possible alterations for accessibility in your home. 

Expand Doorways and Hallways
Using a wheelchair requires more space for moving, and thus, to make your home more wheelchair-friendly, you need to widen the doorways and hallways. This will help improve accessibility at home and allow you to move freely. It also contributes to a seamless arrangement in the living space. 

Installation of Ramp
If your house is at a height with stairs to reach the base, the same may not be feasible for people with wheelchairs. Thus, it is important that you install ramps at entry points for easy movement for people using wheelchairs. This eliminates the need to climb and descend the stairs further, allowing a smooth transition.
 
Slip-Proof Floors
Non-slip floors lessen the risks of accidents and injuries. Especially in houses that are exposed to damp conditions, their kitchens and bathrooms are more prone to be slippery, raising the chances of accidents and slips. Thus, people must deploy anti-slip floors as a practical option to prevent such mishaps, in addition to being an aesthetically pleasing introduction. 

Accessible Room Furnishings
Furniture and furnishings usually take a back seat while talking about making homes accessible for wheelchair individuals. But, you need to know that the furniture arrangement also matters the most because wrongly arranged furniture can lead to mishaps. You should place the furniture such that it allows free movement and clear pathways. This facilitates manoeuvrability and also crafts the space to be more inviting and spacious.

Incorporation of Smart Home Technology
If your budget permits, you can also consider deploying smart gadgets and technology in your home to enhance the ease of living for disabled people. Automated lighting systems, voice assistants, and similar technologies can boost practicality and overall liability.
