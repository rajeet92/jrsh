Travel Tips for Wheelchair Users Flying Out of Delhi

Traveling by plane involves multiple steps that can be incredibly challenging for wheelchair users. Many people who face mobility issues get cold feet while traveling abroad on planes. Thankfully, little early preparation can save them from many hurdles in the future. To help make your journey from Delhi smoother, here are eight crucial tips along with common problems wheelchair users face and their solutions. Through this blog, we want to help wheelchair users plan their traveling in a way so that they can enjoy the journey more, 

Travel Tips for Wheelchair Users

Here are some crucial tips that will help you eliminate traveling blues. These tips are given below: 

Arrive Early
Arriving three hours early for international flights and two hours early for domestic flights is advised. If you're traveling in a wheelchair, consider adding extra time to navigate check-in and security, as it may take longer to process any medical equipment or medications.

Carry Your Seat Cushion
Before your flight, remember to remove the seat cushion from your wheelchair and bring it on the plane with you. This simple step not only enhances your comfort during the flight but also ensures the safety of your cushion, preventing any potential damage or loss in the cargo hold.

Verify Tag Information
Ensure that the gate check tag on your wheelchair correctly reflects your destination details. This simple double-check is crucial as it ensures that your wheelchair is loaded onto the correct aircraft and prevents it from being misplaced. A small mistake in the tag information can lead to your wheelchair being sent to the wrong destination, causing significant inconvenience.

Label Your Wheelchair
To ensure the safety of your wheelchair, it's important to personalize it with your name and contact information. Use a durable tag that’s securely attached to prevent any chance of it getting torn off. This personal touch not only helps in identifying your wheelchair but also makes you feel valued and considered.

Remove and Carry Detachable Parts
Detach any removable parts like side guards before handing over your wheelchair for the flight. These parts can usually be removed by unscrewing or unclipping them from the frame. Once removed, carry them on the plane with you in a secure bag or pouch. This reduces the risk of damage or loss during transport.

Locate Wheelchair Services
Before you leave, get information from your local provider about wheelchair repair services at your destination. This can be done by asking your local provider for recommendations or doing a quick online search for wheelchair repair services in the area. This preparation will help you deal with any unexpected issues during your trip and ensure that you have access to the necessary support if needed.

Inquire About Pre-boarding
Many airlines provide the option to pre-board for passengers who need additional time to settle in comfortably. To inquire about this service, you can either call the airline's customer service or ask at the check-in counter when you arrive at the airport. Always ask if this service is available and what the process is to ensure a smooth boarding experience.

Provide Instructions for Your Electric Wheelchair
If you use an electric wheelchair, attach a note with detailed instructions on handling it, especially about engaging and disengaging the brakes.

Traveling in a wheelchair from Delhi can be a rewarding experience with the proper preparation. Remember, these tips are just the beginning. As you travel more, you’ll find specific strategies that work best for you.
